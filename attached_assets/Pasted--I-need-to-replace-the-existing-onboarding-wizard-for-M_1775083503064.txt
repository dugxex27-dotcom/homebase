
"I need to replace the existing onboarding wizard for MyHomeBase with an interactive guided tour that walks new users through the actual app using highlighted tooltips, arrows, and step by step guidance. This should feel like a friendly guide walking alongside them through their new home management platform — not a form or questionnaire.
The tour should use a spotlight effect that dims everything on screen except the element being highlighted. An arrow points to the highlighted element. A tooltip appears next to it with a brief friendly explanation and a next button to advance to the following step. The user can tap the highlighted element and actually interact with it during the tour rather than just reading about it.
Here is the complete step by step flow for the guided tour.
Step one — Welcome Screen
Before the tour begins show a single full screen welcome card with the MyHomeBase logo. If the homeowner was referred by a real estate agent show their agent's name and photo from the agent card. Display the message — Welcome to MyHomeBase. Your personal home management platform. We will show you around in about 3 minutes. Two buttons appear — Let's Go which starts the tour and Skip Tour which goes directly to the dashboard.
Step two — Your Home Health Score
Spotlight highlights the Home Health Score circle on the dashboard. Arrow points directly at it. Tooltip says — This is your Home Health Score. It starts building the moment you complete your first task. The higher your score the better documented your home is for insurance and resale purposes. Tap the score to see more details. User taps the score and sees the score detail screen. Next button advances the tour.
Step three — Adding Your Property Details
Spotlight highlights the property address card on the dashboard. Arrow points to the Edit or Setup button. Tooltip says — This is your home profile. Tap here to add your home's mechanical features like your HVAC, roof, and plumbing. The more details you add the more personalized your maintenance tasks become. If the agent already entered the address show it populated and say — Your agent has already added your address. You can add more details here anytime. User taps through to the property detail screen. Highlight the key fields one at a time. Arrow points to heating system field. Tooltip says — Tap here to select your heating system type. You can find this information on your home inspection report or on the unit itself usually on a label inside the front panel. Arrow points to the approximate age dropdown. Tooltip says — Not sure of the age? Check your home inspection report or look for a manufacture date sticker on the unit. You can always add this later. User makes selections or skips. Next button advances.
Step four — Your Monthly Maintenance Tasks
Spotlight highlights the maintenance task list. Arrow points to the first task card. Tooltip says — Every month we give you a personalized list of maintenance tasks based on your home's location, climate, and mechanical features. These are the tasks that keep your home healthy and your insurance claim protected. Arrow points to the priority badge on the task. Tooltip says — Tasks are color coded by priority. Red is high priority and should be done this month. Yellow is medium priority. Green is low priority but still important. Arrow points to the cost range on the task card. Tooltip says — We show you the estimated cost so you can decide whether to DIY or hire a contractor. Arrow points to the View Details button. Tooltip says — Tap View Details to see step by step instructions for completing this task yourself or to find a contractor who can do it for you. User taps View Details and sees the task detail screen. Next button advances.
Step five — Completing a Task
Spotlight highlights the complete task button or checkbox on the task detail screen. Arrow points directly at it. Tooltip says — When you finish a task tap here to mark it complete. Your Home Health Score goes up with every task you complete. Want to try it? Let them mark the sample task complete and show the score animation increasing. Tooltip says — Great work. Every completed task builds your verified maintenance record. Next button advances.
Step six — Service Records
Spotlight highlights the Service Records item in the navigation menu. Arrow points to it. Tooltip says — This is where your complete home maintenance history lives. Every task you complete and every contractor visit gets logged here automatically. Arrow points to the Add Service Record button. Tooltip says — You can also add your own records manually. Had your gutters cleaned last month? Log it here with the date cost and any photos. Arrow points to the Download by Date and Download by Area buttons. Tooltip says — At any time you can download your complete service history sorted by date or by area of your home. This is the document that protects your insurance claim. Next button advances.
Step seven — Adding Appliances
Spotlight highlights the appliances section or navigation item. Arrow points to it. Tooltip says — Add your home appliances here with their model and serial numbers. We will store your owner's manuals and alert you to any recalls or warranty expirations. Arrow points to the Add Appliance button. Tooltip says — You can find model and serial numbers on a sticker inside the door or on the back of most appliances. Your home inspection report may also list them. Tap here to add your first appliance or skip this for now and come back later. Next button advances.
Step eight — Finding Contractors
Spotlight highlights the Contractors section in the navigation menu. Arrow points to it. Tooltip says — Find verified contractors in your area here. Every contractor on MyHomeBase is linked to real service records and verified reviews from homeowners just like you. Arrow points to the search field. Tooltip says — Search by service type or location to find contractors near you. When you hire a contractor through MyHomeBase they can upload your service records directly to your home history. Next button advances.
Step nine — Your Achievements
Spotlight highlights the Achievements section. Arrow points to it. Tooltip says — Earn medals and achievements as you complete tasks and build your Home Health Score. Complete all your tasks in a month to earn a Perfect Month medal. Keep completing tasks every month to build your streak. Arrow points to the referral section. Tooltip says — Share MyHomeBase with friends and family using your personal referral code. You both get a free month when they sign up and stay active. Next button advances.
Step ten — Tour Complete
Show a congratulations full screen card. Display their current Home Health Score prominently. Show a summary — You are all set. Here is what to do next as three simple action items. Complete your first maintenance task this month. Add your home's mechanical features for more personalized tasks. Invite your contractor to join MyHomeBase so they can upload your service records directly. Show a single prominent button — Go To My Dashboard. Show a secondary option — Share MyHomeBase with a friend using your referral code with social sharing buttons optimized per platform.
Technical requirements for the tour:
Use a library like Shepherd.js or Intro.js to build the tooltip and spotlight system. These are well established JavaScript libraries for building product tours. The spotlight effect should dim the background to about 50% opacity so the highlighted element is clearly visible. Arrows should animate subtly to draw attention. The tour progress should be saved after each step so if a user closes the app mid tour they return to where they left off with a banner saying Continue Your Setup. The tour should only trigger once for new users. Add a Replay Tour option in the Account settings so users can revisit it anytime. All tour elements should work perfectly on mobile screens with touch friendly tap targets. Maintain the existing purple and green design language throughout the tour. The tour should work whether or not the agent has pre-populated the property information — if information is already there highlight it and explain what it is rather than asking them to enter it.
Please build this complete interactive guided tour and replace the existing onboarding wizard entirely.
