I need to build a home inspection document upload and onboarding wizard into MyHomeBase. This feature serves two purposes — allowing existing users to upload their home inspection report for reference, and guiding new users through entering their home information during initial signup.
Part One — Home Inspection Document Upload
Build a document upload section in the homeowner dashboard under a new section called Home Documents.
The upload feature should accept PDF and image files in JPG and PNG format up to 50MB in size.
When a user uploads a home inspection document use AWS Textract to automatically extract key information from the document including the following fields. Property address. Inspection date. Inspector name and license number. Roof age and condition. HVAC system age and condition. Electrical panel type and condition. Plumbing condition. Foundation condition. Water heater age and condition. Any major deficiencies or items flagged as needing immediate attention. Any items flagged as needing monitoring or future attention.
After extraction display the extracted information to the user in a clean review screen showing what the system found. Allow the user to confirm, edit, or add to any extracted field before saving. Fields that could not be extracted should be highlighted so the user knows what to fill in manually.
Once confirmed automatically populate the relevant fields in their home profile including mechanical features, appliance ages, and system conditions. Create a service record entry noting the home inspection date and inspector information as the baseline starting point for their maintenance history.
Store the original uploaded document in the user's Home Documents vault so they can access and download it at any time.
Display a home inspection summary card on their dashboard showing inspection date, inspector name, and a count of any flagged items that need attention.
Any flagged deficiency items from the inspection should automatically generate custom maintenance tasks in the user's task list with appropriate priority levels. Critical items should be marked high priority. Monitor items should be marked medium priority.
Part Two — New User Onboarding Wizard
Build a step by step onboarding wizard that appears the first time a new user signs up. The wizard should feel welcoming and simple not overwhelming. Use a progress bar showing which step they are on out of the total steps.
Step one is Welcome. Display a welcome message explaining what MyHomeBase does in three simple bullet points. Protect your home. Document your maintenance. Build your Home Health Score. Include a brief explanation that the whole setup takes about five minutes. Include two options as large buttons. Upload My Home Inspection Report which takes them to the document upload flow from Part One. Enter My Home Info Manually which takes them to step two.
Step two is Property Address. Ask for their full property address. Validate the address using SmartyStreets API to confirm it is a real US address. Auto detect and display their climate zone based on the address. Show them a confirmation that says something like Your home is in the Pacific Northwest climate zone — we will customize your maintenance tasks based on your local weather and conditions.
Step three is Home Type and Basic Features. Ask simple yes or no questions with toggle switches for the following. Do you have a pool? Do you have a garage? Do you have a basement? Do you have solar panels? Do you have a deck or patio? Is this a primary residence, vacation home, or investment property?
Step four is Home Systems. Ask about their major mechanical systems with simple dropdown selectors. Heating system type — gas furnace, oil furnace, heat pump, electric baseboard, or other. Do you know the approximate age? Dropdown of less than 5 years, 5 to 10 years, 10 to 15 years, 15 to 20 years, over 20 years, or not sure. Cooling system — central air, window units, mini split, or none. Do you know the approximate age? Same age dropdown. Water heater type — gas, electric, tankless, or not sure. Approximate age using same dropdown. Roof type — asphalt shingles, metal, tile, flat, or not sure. Approximate age using same dropdown. Electrical panel — standard breaker box, fuse box, or not sure.
All age and type fields should be optional with a clearly visible Skip for Now option on every question so users do not feel blocked by information they do not have readily available. Show a tooltip on each question explaining why this information helps them — for example Knowing your roof age helps us remind you when to start budgeting for replacement and flag it for insurance documentation.
Step five is Appliances. Ask if they want to add their major appliances now. Explain that adding model and serial numbers helps track warranties and get recalled product alerts. Show a simple add appliance form with fields for appliance type, brand, model number, serial number, and purchase or installation date. Allow them to add multiple appliances or skip entirely with a Skip for Now button. Include a note that they can always add appliances later from their dashboard.
Step six is Contractors. Ask if they have any contractors they already use regularly. Explain that linking contractors allows them to upload service records directly to your home. Show a search field to find contractors already on the platform by name or business name. Allow them to skip if they do not have contractors yet or if their contractors are not on the platform yet. Include a note that they can invite their contractors to join MyHomeBase from their dashboard.
Step seven is Completion. Show a congratulations screen with their initial Home Health Score displayed prominently in the circular score display. Show a summary of what they set up — number of systems configured, number of appliances added, climate zone detected. Show their first month's maintenance tasks as a preview so they immediately see the value. Display a single prominent call to action button that says Go To My Dashboard. Include a secondary option to share MyHomeBase with a friend using their referral code with social sharing buttons optimized for each platform.

Build a Home Documents section accessible from the main navigation menu between Service Records and Messages.
