Why Claims Get Denied

42% of claims are denied or underpaid.

That's not a rounding error — it's nearly every other homeowner who files a claim.

#1

No maintenance records

Insurers require proof that the affected system was properly maintained. Without service history, they assume neglect — and deny.

#2

Late or incomplete documentation

Receipts, contractor invoices, and inspection reports submitted after the fact are often rejected. Timing and completeness matter.

#3

Pre-existing condition ruling

Without a dated maintenance log, adjusters can claim the damage was pre-existing — shifting the burden of proof to you.

#4

Policy exclusions triggered by neglect

Most homeowner policies explicitly exclude damage resulting from deferred maintenance. One missed service call can void coverage.
